Big Data Final Project

New York City Coronavirus Data Analysis

Team Member: Zhi Zhang, Jessie Jin, Xuexuan Wang, Jenny Zhou

About this Project

As we are living in unprecedented times of pandemic, our team would like to put our efforts and contribute to battling COVID-19 health crisis. This project aims to help track and analyze the current COVID pandemic spread in NYC across the five boroughs: Manhattan, Queens, Brooklyn, and Staten Island. In addition, we included studies of relations between pandemic and its influential factors: age, income and traffic. Moreover, we analyzed impacted areas due to the burst of the virus: job market, business license application, certificate of occupancy issued for new buildings, public transportation, emergency notification for public health, hospital icu beds and permitted events. Based on current data, we forecasted the number of positive cases using machine learning algorithms.

  • Positive tests tracking map
  • Infections and fatalities analysis
  • Influential factors: Who is getting sick
  • Impacted areas: job market, business license application, certificate of occupancy issued for new buildings, traffic collison, emergency notification for public health, hospital icu beds and permitted events
Data:
  • NYC Department of Health and Mental Hygiene (DOHMH)
  • New York State Department of Health
  • THE CITY
  • NYC Open Data
Methodology:
  • Cases Prediction: Locistic Increase Model with Least Squares Approximation

Who is Getting Sick

We analyzed three factors that would affect the positive population:

  • Age: teenager and middle-aged group has the highest case number. But the number of deaths and death rate are highest in senior citizen.
  • Income: the low-income group has the largest number of COVID-19 positive cases.
  • Traffic: boroughs with more traffics have the higher number of COVID-19 infections.

—— How Income Affects The Positive Popilation ——


The table below shows the relationship between COVID-19 positive case and income. The number of low-income units (blue part) gradually increases on the x-axis. The latter half of the x-axis shows that COVID-19 positive cases (orange part) is also increasing with the increase of number of low-income units.




According to the income, we divide the data into three groups. The result shows that the positive cases from the low-income group is much more than the middle-income group and moderate-income group.



—— How Traffic Affects The Positive Popilation ——



The bar graphs below show the number of car crashes and number of COVID-19 cases acrossing the five boroughs. Queens and Brooklyn have the highest number of infections also have the highest number of car crashes. Since data on nyc traffics are limited, number of car crashes is used to feflect the traffics. This shows that the areas where have more traffics tend to have more COVID-19 cases.

COVID-19 Impact on NYC

We analyze COVID-19 Impact on job market, business license application, certificate of occupancy issued for new buildings, public transportation, emergency notification for public health, and permitted events.
Compare with the average two months ago, the result shows that the following areas are declining: job application(down 36.13%), business license application(down 86.98%), certificate of occupancy issued for new buildings(down 33.43%), traffic collison(down 74.90%), 2020 NYC permitted events(average down by 98.76% per month). While the following is increasing: emergency notification for public health(up 1362.50%) and hospital icu bed numbers (up 77.23%).
