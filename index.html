NYC Coronavirus Data Analysis

Big Data Final Project

Team Member: Jessie Jin, Zhi Zhang, Xuexuan Wang, Jenny Zhou

About this Project

As we are living in unprecedented times of pandemic, our team would like to put our efforts and contribute to battling COVID-19 health crisis. This project aims to help track and analyze the current COVID pandemic spread in NYC across the five boroughs: Manhattan, Queens, Brooklyn, and Staten Island. In addition, we included studies of relations between pandemic and its influential factors: age, income and traffic. Moreover, we analyzed impacted areas due to the burst of the virus: job market, business license application, certificate of occupancy issued for new buildings, public transportation, emergency notification for public health, hospital icu beds and permitted events. Based on current data, we forecasted the number of positive cases using machine learning algorithms.

  • Positive tests tracking map
  • Infections and fatalities analysis
  • Influential factors: Who is getting sick
  • Impacted areas: job market, business license application, certificate of occupancy issued for new buildings, traffic collision, emergency notification for public health, hospital icu beds and permitted events
Big Data Technology:
  • Spark and Pandas: data processing and analysis
  • SparkML: k-means algorithm for anomaly detection
  • Cases Prediction: logistic increase model with least squares approximation
  • D3.js big data visualization

Social Factors Affecting COVID-19

We analyzed three factors that would affect the COVID-19 case:

  • Age: adult and middle-aged group has the highest positive case number. The number of deaths and death rate are highest in senior citizens.
  • Income: the low-income group has the largest number of COVID-19 cases.
  • Traffic: boroughs with more traffics have the higher number of COVID-19 cases.

The chart below shows the relationship between COVID-19 case and age. Adult and middle-aged group has the highest case number. But the number of deaths and death rate are highest in senior citizens.

—— How Traffic Affects The COVID-19 Case ——



The bar graphs below show the number of car crashes and number of COVID-19 cases acrossing the five boroughs. Queens and Brooklyn have the highest number of infections also have the highest number of car crashes. Since data on nyc traffics are limited, number of car crashes is used to reflect the traffic. This shows that the areas where have more traffics tend to have more COVID-19 cases.

COVID-19 Impacts on NYC

We analyzed COVID-19 Impacts on the job market, business license application, certificate of occupancy issued for new buildings, public transportation, emergency notification for public health, and permitted events.

Comparing with the 6-month average prior to pandemic, the results show that the following areas are declining:

  • Job application down by: 39.19%
  • Business license application down by: 86.71%
  • Certificate of occupancy issued for new buildings down by: 33.43%
  • Traffic collision down by: 74.77%
  • 2020 NYC permitted events down by: 98.73% per month
